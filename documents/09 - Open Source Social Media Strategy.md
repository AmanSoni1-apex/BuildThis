## 1. Why Open Source?

Open-sourcing BuildThis.dev is a **strategic move**, especially as a student founder aiming for YC.

**A. Vercel for Open Source:**
Vercel offers **free, unlimited hosting/bandwidth** for open-source projects. This means your $0 infrastructure plan is guaranteed forever.

**B. Developer Trust:**
Developers love to see how things work. Open-sourcing builds trust‚Äîthey know you aren't just farming their data.

**C. Community Contributions:**
Other developers might help you build features, fix bugs, or even suggest new "real-world problems" to add to the bank.

**D. Ultimate Portfolio Piece:**
A high-quality, open-source project with stars and contributors is the best resume you can ever have.

## 2. Setting Up for Open Source

### A. The License
**Recommended:** **MIT License**.
- It‚Äôs the most permissive and common for startups.
- Anyone can use the code, for any purpose, but they can't sue you.

### B. The README.md
Your GitHub README is your "Storefront." It MUST include:
- A clear value proposition (Why does this exist?).
- A link to the live demo (buildthis.vercel.app).
- A section on "How to Contribute."
- Credits to you as the founder.

## 3. Social Media Strategy

**YES, you should post!** Traction is the most important metric for YC.

### A. Hacker News (The Holy Grail)
- **Target:** "Show HN" section.
- **Tip:** Be humble and engineering-focused.
- **Template Title:** `Show HN: BuildThis.dev ‚Äì A problem bank for developers, not tutorials`
- **Post Content:** Explain why you built it‚Äîyou were tired of "Todo list" apps and wanted real-world problems.

- **Subreddits:** r/programming, r/sideproject, r/selfhosted, r/webdev.

- **Template Title:** `I‚Äôm a student and I built a platform to help developers find real-world problems to solve. I‚Äôd love your feedback!`

### C. X/Twitter (Viral Potential)

- **Tip:** Post screenshots and video demos. Talk about the "AI Refinement" feature‚Äîit‚Äôs very "X" friendly.

Once you post, you need to manage the noise:
